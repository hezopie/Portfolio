Careers Site

Opportunity

With company expansion, recruiting new talent became a primary focus.

Research

The team started by visiting top career sites and gathered ideas and studied best practices. We continued hosting collaborative meetings with the recruitment team to walk through the hiring process in great detail, from application to orientation.

We then collected data and documented areas that could be improved or needed additional research from both a candidate and a recruiter perspective, set up a dashboard in Google analytics to set measurable goals, and added Crazy Egg to evaluate user behavior on the existing career page. We even had a team member participate in a career fair to observe and gather info.

Our determination was that a full careers site made more sense than the single page we currently had. This careers site could direct visitors to more relevant job info, bring a well-informed applicant for their first interview, serve up content that represents the company as a great place to work, build a drip campaign with more targeted content to anyone that signed up for more information, and answer the commonly asked questions ahead of time.

My Role

I spearheaded project research, information gathering, documentation and management, detailed hours and cost, team meetings with recruiters, brainstorming sessions and summaries, UX/UI support on wireframes, task assignment and QC

Outcome

This project is by far the best research we have done on a project so far. The interaction with HR recruiters, observing real-live applicants and deep-diving into analytics taught us a lot of amazing things about visitors. This initiative is still in progress for completion summer of 2019.
