Brainstorming

Solve problems and finalize ideas

Brainstorming allows my team to evaluate all collected research and requirements, and begin to form conclusions and develop solutions. I try to include as many members of the team as I can in this step. Depending on what we are trying to solve, more often than not, everyone walks away with an assigned task or two, and/or research to followup on. The more engaged team members are, the more vested they are.

I have one steadfast rule for brainstorming… stay stocked up on sticky notes!

I love to see participants sharing well beyond the brainstorming meeting (that’s when I know a meeting is a productive one). Then there are those times that the ideas are just not flowing. Then it’s time to send the team out for a break to get a cup of joe… and try again later.
